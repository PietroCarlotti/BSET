We are pleased to submit the attached manuscript ``A Bayesian Critique of a Rank-Based Method for Surrogate Marker Evaluation" to be considered for publication in the Biometric Methodology section of Biometrics. In this paper, we propose BSET (Bayesian Surrogate Evaluation Test), a Bayesian imputation-based method for assessing the validity of surrogate markers in clinical trials. Specifically, we propose a method to address key limitations of the existing rank-based approach of Parast et al. (2024), including its lack of causal interpretability, low statistical power, and inability to adjust for baseline covariates. We derive the analytical forms of both the estimand targeted by Parast et al. (2024) and the causal estimand targeted by BSET under Gaussian data-generating mechanisms, demonstrating that the two can differ substantially and that the rank-based estimand can yield misleading conclusions about surrogate validity even in relatively simple settings. Our approach accommodates both settings with and without baseline covariates, and provides a new criterion for calibrating the surrogate validation threshold based on a Bayes factor argument. We investigate the performance of our method relative to the existing frequentist approach using a simulation study and illustrate our procedures by examining the validity of an early measurement of HbA1c as a surrogate for a later measurement using data from the Diabetes Control and Complications Trial (DCCT).

We believe our findings would be very appealing to your readers and would be a useful contribution to the field of surrogate markers.

A preliminary version of this work was posted as an arXiv preprint (arXiv:2603.14381). The submitted manuscript is a substantially revised and expanded version of that preprint, incorporating additional theoretical results, an extended simulation study, and a more detailed application section.
